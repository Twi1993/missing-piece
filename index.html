FAQ

Common Questions

Is WGU actually accredited?
Yes. WGU is regionally accredited by the Northwest Commission on Colleges and Universities (NWCCU) - the same type of accreditation as traditional state universities. Employers and graduate programs recognize it.
Can I really finish in 4 months?
Troy did it. Others in the WGU community have done it in similar or shorter timeframes. It depends on how aggressively you work through Sophia and how quickly you demonstrate competency at WGU. The guide gives you the system - your pace determines your timeline.
What does it actually cost?
Sophia Learning is $99/month. One WGU semester runs roughly $3,755–$4,500 depending on your program. Pell Grant recipients may have WGU costs covered entirely. Total realistic out-of-pocket: $200–$5,000.
Do I need to quit my job?
No. Both Sophia and WGU are fully online and self-paced. Troy completed this while working full time. Evenings and weekends are enough.
How did Troy get his degree for under $500?
Troy's out-of-pocket cost was under $500 because he qualified for the Pell Grant, which covered most of his WGU tuition. Without financial aid, expect $4,000-$5,000 total. The guide includes a full section on maximizing financial aid - for eligible students, WGU can cost almost nothing.
What degrees can I get this way?
The guide covers Business Administration, Healthcare/Health & Human Services, IT, Cybersecurity, and more. Business degrees have the most transfer-friendly pathway with no 2026 restrictions.
What if I've never heard of WGU or Sophia?
The guide assumes zero prior knowledge. It explains both platforms, how transfers work, and walks you through every step from account creation to graduation application.
